Amma's sambar

Sambar is a popular South Indian dish made using lentils (usually toor dal), tamarind, vegetables, and a blend of aromatic spices. It is known for its tangy and mildly spicy taste and is commonly served with rice, idli, dosa, vada, and pongal. Rich in nutrients and flavor, sambar is an important part of traditional South Indian cuisine.

Ingredients

  • Toor dal
  • Tamarind
  • Onion
  • Tomato
  • Drumstick
  • Sambar powder

Instructions

  1. Wash and pressure-cook toor dal until soft.
  2. Chop vegetables like onion, tomato, and drumstick.
  3. Boil the vegetables with tamarind water until cooked.
  4. Add sambar powder and salt, then mix well.
  5. Add the cooked dal and simmer for a few minutes.
  6. Prepare a tempering with mustard seeds and curry leaves, then add it to the sambar.
